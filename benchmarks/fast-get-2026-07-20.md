errors. The lowest paired throughput ratio was 0.985 and the lowest paired p99

The safe winner is selected by median throughput. Ratios are the median of the
per-round automatic/safe throughput ratio and safe/automatic p99 ratio. Values
above one favor automatic. The complete per-round throughput, p50, p95, p99,
p99.9, process CPU, allocated bytes/op, GC, misses, concurrent writes, and
errors are in `fast-get-2026-07-20.csv`.

## Method

- Real RocksDB and a generated stock gRPC client over IPv4 loopback.
- Four concurrent readers, a rate-limited 500 writes/s writer, 10% misses, a
  500 ms warmup, and a 2 s measurement window for every sample.
- Five alternating-order rounds for every band. The 256-byte and mixed-small
  selector recheck used seven alternating rounds.
- Correctness sizes: missing, 0, 1, 32, 256, 1024, 4096, 65536, and 2097152
  bytes, plus mixed-small and mixed-wide distributions.
- A transient host-wide scheduling/write stall affected all strategies and the
  benchmark writer together in three bands of the initial matrix. Those bands
  were rerun, and mixed-wide was rerun because the final 256-byte selector also
  affects that distribution; unaffected rows were retained. The composite CSV
  contains at least five rounds for every strategy and band.

The primary command was:

```shell
taskset -c 4-11 mvn -q -DskipTests test-compile \
  org.codehaus.mojo:exec-maven-plugin:3.5.0:java \
  -Dexec.mainClass=it.cavallium.rockserver.core.impl.benchmark.GrpcFastGetBenchmark \
  -Dexec.classpathScope=test \
  -Dexec.args='--rounds=5 --warmup-ms=500 --measure-ms=2000 \
    --readers=4 --writer-rate=500 --sample-every=8 \
    --regression-limit=0.03'
```

The 256-byte and mixed-small recheck changed `--rounds=7` and selected those
bands with `--bands=256,mixed-small`.
